Section 01

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to website owners.

Cookies do not contain executable code and cannot carry viruses. They are stored by your browser and can be read only by the website that created them (or in some cases, by authorised third-party services).

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time by clicking "Manage Preferences" in the cookie banner at the bottom of any page on our website.

Section 03

Types of Cookies We Use

Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function and cannot be disabled. They are usually set in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in, or filling in forms. You can set your browser to block these cookies, but the website may not function correctly as a result.

Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. This allows us to improve our website and user experience. You can opt out of analytics cookies via our cookie preferences panel.

Marketing Cookies

Marketing cookies are used to track visitors across websites so that relevant advertisements can be shown. They are set by third-party advertising partners. These cookies are disabled by default and are only activated with your consent.

Functional Cookies

Functional cookies enable enhanced features such as live chat, embedded videos, and personalised content. Disabling these may affect the functionality of certain features on our website.

Section 06

Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

Using our Cookie Preference Centre

Click "Manage Preferences" in the cookie banner at the bottom of our website to control which categories of cookies you allow. Your preferences are saved and respected on future visits.

Through your browser settings

Most browsers allow you to refuse or delete cookies. Here are links to instructions for common browsers:

  • Google Chrome — Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies
  • Mozilla Firefox — Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies
  • Safari — Preferences > Privacy > Cookies
  • Microsoft Edge — Settings > Privacy > Cookies

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website, including the ability to add items to your cart or complete a purchase.
